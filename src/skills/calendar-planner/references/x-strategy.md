# X/Twitter Growth Strategy

## Phase Definitions

### Phase 0: Profile Setup (Day 1)
- **Trigger:** Account just created or has no optimized bio
- **Goal:** Establish credibility and discoverability
- **Actions:**
  - Name format: `{Name} | Building {Product}`
  - Bio: One line — what the product does + who it's for + #buildinpublic
  - Pinned tweet: Best build-in-public moment or product demo GIF
  - Banner: Product screenshot with URL
  - Link: Direct to product URL (no Linktree, no friction)
- **No calendar entries for this phase** — profile setup only

### Phase 1: Reply Phase (0-500 followers)
- **Trigger:** `followerCount < 500`
- **Goal:** Build initial audience through high-value replies to bigger accounts
- **Daily workflow:** 80% replying, 20% posting
- **Original posts per day:** 1-2
- **Threads per week:** 1 (5-8 tweets)
- **Content mix for original posts:**
  - 50% metric (build-in-public updates with real numbers)
  - 30% educational (lessons learned, tips)
  - 15% engagement (questions, polls)
  - 5% product (only demos, never pitches)
- **Posting times (UTC):**
  - Morning: 13:00 (9 AM EST)
  - Afternoon: 17:00 (1 PM EST)
- **Hashtags:** #buildinpublic on every post + 1-2 from #indiehackers, #saas, #aitools, #microsaas
- **Key rules:**
  - Never pitch the product in replies. Ever.
  - Reply within 15 minutes of target account posts
  - Add real value in replies: experience, data points, contrarian takes
  - Engage with every reply received that day before bed

### Phase 2: Content Phase (500-2,000 followers)
- **Trigger:** `followerCount >= 500 && followerCount < 2000`
- **Goal:** Shift to original content creation while maintaining engagement
- **Daily workflow:** 50% replying, 50% original content
- **Original posts per day:** 2-3
- **Threads per week:** 1-2
- **Content mix:**
  - 40% metric (build-in-public updates, revenue, users, decisions, struggles)
  - 30% educational (tips, frameworks, lessons learned)
  - 20% engagement (questions, polls, hot takes)
  - 10% product (demos, features, launches)
- **Posting times (UTC):**
  - Morning: 13:00 (9 AM EST)
  - Midday: 17:00 (1 PM EST)
  - Afternoon: 21:00 (5 PM EST)
- **Community strategy:** Post in X Communities (Indie Hackers, Build in Public, SaaS Founders, Startup Community)
- **DM strategy:** DM 5 founders/week you've been engaging with — relationships, not pitches

### Phase 3: Launch Playbook (user-triggered)
- **Trigger:** User explicitly activates launch mode
- **Goal:** Maximize launch visibility and conversion
- **Pre-launch (2 weeks before):**
  - Tease the problem being solved
  - Share mockups, demos, early results
  - "Drop a reply if you want early access" tweet
  - DM 30-50 engaged followers offering beta access
- **Launch day (Tuesday-Thursday, 13:00 UTC / 9 AM EST):**
  - Launch thread (7-8 tweets): Hook > Problem > Solution (with GIF) > Key features > Social proof > Behind the scenes > Pricing > CTA with link
  - Put the link in the LAST tweet only (algorithm suppresses link tweets)
  - Share in all X Communities joined
  - Ask warm contacts to RT the first tweet
  - Reply to every comment all day
- **Post-launch:**
  - Day 2: "24 hours later, here are the numbers"
  - Week 1: Individual feature highlights
  - Week 2: Retrospective thread

### Phase 4: Ongoing Growth Engine (2,000+ followers)
- **Trigger:** `followerCount >= 2000`
- **Goal:** Compound growth through consistent high-quality content
- **Daily workflow:** 30% replying, 70% original content
- **Original posts per day:** 3+
- **Threads per week:** 2+
- **Content mix:**
  - 35% metric (milestones, numbers, build-in-public)
  - 30% educational (deep insights, frameworks)
  - 20% engagement (discussions, hot takes, community)
  - 15% product (features, demos, case studies)
- **Posting times (UTC):**
  - Morning: 12:00 (8 AM EST)
  - Late morning: 14:00 (10 AM EST)
  - Afternoon: 17:00 (1 PM EST)
  - Evening: 21:00 (5 PM EST)
- **Growth tactics:**
  - Milestone posts for every win: "100 users!", "First paying customer!", "$1K MRR!"
  - Quote-tweet exchanges with founders at similar level
  - Repurpose: Top tweets > threads > blog posts > tweet series
  - Track X Analytics weekly, double down on formats with most bookmarks

## Universal Rules

### Do
- Lead with value, stories, lessons
- Use 1-3 hashtags, #buildinpublic always
- Post link as first reply to your own tweet (not in main tweet)
- Be authentic, share failures too
- Reply to every comment on your posts
- Post consistently every day
- Use numbers and specifics over vague claims
- Sound human — write like you're texting a smart friend

### Don't
- Post only about your product
- Use 5+ hashtags (looks like a bot)
- Put links directly in tweets (50%+ reach penalty)
- Be corporate or polished
- Ignore engagement on your own content
- Go viral once then disappear for 2 weeks
- Use AI-sounding language ("leverage", "delve", "comprehensive")
- Overuse emojis (max 1-2 per tweet)

## Thread Best Practices

- **Hook tweet (Tweet #1):** Must stop the scroll. Specific, slightly provocative, promises value.
- **Body tweets (#2-6):** One idea per tweet. Short sentences. White space. No filler.
- **Closing tweet:** Call to action or summary. Link goes in FIRST REPLY, not in the thread.
- **Best thread topics:**
  - "I built X this week. Here's what I learned:"
  - Revenue/user number updates with screenshots
  - A specific technical or marketing lesson
  - "X things I wish I knew before building Y"
  - Comparison/analysis threads with real data

## Algorithm Signals (2025-2026)

Priority ranking of engagement signals:
1. **Bookmarks** — highest weight, signals "save for later" value
2. **Replies** — conversation signals quality content
3. **Retweets** — distribution signal
4. **Likes** — baseline engagement, lowest weight
5. **Link clicks** — but links in tweets are PENALIZED for reach

Optimize for bookmarks and replies above all else.
