# Community Signal Reference

## What Makes a Community Worth Targeting

### High-Value Signals

- **Active solution-seeking**: Posts asking "what tool do you use for X?" or
  "looking for alternatives to Y" appear regularly
- **Pain point density**: Community members frequently discuss the problem
  your product solves
- **Engagement depth**: Threads get substantive replies, not just upvotes
- **Moderate size**: 10k-500k subscribers — large enough for reach, small
  enough that posts get seen
- **Receptive to recommendations**: History of product recommendations that
  get upvoted, not removed

### Low-Value Signals

- **Too large** (>1M subscribers): Posts get buried quickly
- **Too small** (<1k subscribers): Not enough audience
- **Strict no-promotion rules**: Even genuine recommendations get removed
- **Meme-heavy**: Low signal-to-noise ratio
- **Inactive**: Last meaningful post >7 days ago

### Platform-Specific Considerations

#### Reddit
- Check subreddit rules for self-promotion policies
- Look at moderator activity level (strict vs. relaxed)
- Check for "What tools do you use?" megathreads
- Flair system indicates content types accepted

#### HackerNews
- Best for developer/startup tools
- "Ask HN" and "Show HN" are distinct engagement modes
- High technical bar — superficial recommendations get downvoted
- No subreddit-style segmentation; relevance comes from topic

#### Long-tail Communities
- dev.to, Indie Hackers, specific forum software (Discourse, etc.)
- Often higher engagement per post than large platforms
- Community norms vary widely — research before engaging
