# Interview patterns

Canonical question shapes per `intent`. Use these as anchors — every
generated script should land in this zone stylistically, even if the
specific wording adapts to the product.

## discovery (pre-product)

Intent: understand the reader's status quo before introducing your
product. No product questions.

Backbone:
1. Walk me through the last time you did X.
2. What part of that took the longest?
3. What did you try before settling on the current approach?
4. What's the one thing you keep meaning to improve but haven't?
5. When did you notice this was a problem worth solving?
6. Who else was involved in how it gets done today?
7. What would have to be true for you to switch approaches?
8. How do you know when it's "done" for the week?
9. What's the smallest version of this that would still work?
10. If you could wave a wand and make one part of this disappear, which?

## activation (signed up, not yet activated)

Intent: find the gap between expected value and first-10-minute
reality.

Backbone:
1. Walk me through what you hoped X would do for you when you signed up.
2. What was the first thing you did after creating your account?
3. Was there a moment you paused or felt lost? Tell me about it.
4. What did you expect to see in the first five minutes?
5. Which feature did you look for first? Did you find it?
6. What was the last thing you did before you stopped?
7. What would need to change for you to come back tomorrow?
8. Did anything feel broken, even if it technically worked?
9. What would make you recommend this to a peer right now?
10. If I could change one thing about the first 10 minutes, what?

## retention (still active)

Intent: learn the workflow users settled into + what would cause them
to churn.

Backbone:
1. Walk me through your typical week with X.
2. Which part do you rely on most?
3. Is there a part you haven't used in a while? Why?
4. What's the first thing you'd miss if X went away?
5. What's the last thing you'd miss?
6. How has the way you use X changed over time?
7. What would need to shift for you to cancel?
8. Have you seen competitors you've considered?
9. How do you describe X when a peer asks?
10. What's the one feature you wish existed?

## win_back (churned)

Intent: understand the specific event or shift that drove churn.

Backbone:
1. Walk me through your last week using X.
2. What did that week feel like, compared to your first weeks?
3. When did you first notice you weren't coming back?
4. What did you switch to, if anything?
5. Was there a specific moment or shortcoming?
6. What would need to change for you to try again?
7. Would you recommend X to a peer today? Why or why not?
8. How does the alternative you chose compare?
9. If we'd reached out a month earlier, what could have made a difference?
10. What should I ask you that I haven't?

## pricing (van Westendorp backbone)

Intent: elicit acceptable price range without anchoring on the current
price.

Backbone (first four mirror van Westendorp; last six build context):
1. At what price would X seem so expensive you wouldn't consider it?
2. At what price would X seem expensive but you'd still consider it?
3. At what price would X seem like a bargain — a great deal?
4. At what price would X seem so cheap that you'd doubt its quality?
5. What price are you paying today for the closest alternative?
6. Who approves purchases like this on your team (if not you)?
7. Is this a monthly budget line or a one-time buy for you?
8. What would justify a 2x price if we offered it?
9. Which features would you cut to get a 50% cheaper tier?
10. What's the most you've paid for a tool like this? What did it do?

## Follow-up pool

Shared across intents — the founder drops these in when an answer is
shallow:

- "Can you tell me about the last time that happened?"
- "What did you do about it that week?"
- "Who else was in the room when that happened?"
- "What was the smallest thing you tried first?"
- "What did you expect would happen?"
- "How did you know it wasn't working?"
- "What did that look like on Monday vs Friday?"
- "What do you wish you'd known going in?"
- "If this were a movie scene, what's the camera on?"
- "Anything I didn't ask that I should have?"

## Anti-patterns (reject all)

- Questions that start with "Do you like..." (yes/no, leading).
- Compound questions with two clauses separated by "and".
- Questions that pitch the product inside the question stem.
- Hypothetical 3-year-out questions ("If we added X in Q4...").
- Open-ended "what do you think about X" with no behavioral anchor.
