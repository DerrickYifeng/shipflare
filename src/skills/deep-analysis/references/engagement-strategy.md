# Engagement Strategy Reference

## Reply Strategy Decision Tree

### reply_to_op
Best when:
- OP is directly asking for recommendations or alternatives
- No existing reply covers your product's use case
- Thread is <48 hours old (for Reddit) or <24 hours (for HN)
- OP has replied to other suggestions (signals active monitoring)

### reply_to_comment
Best when:
- A commenter mentions a competitor or adjacent tool
- Someone asks a follow-up that your product addresses
- The OP's question is too broad but a comment narrows the scope
- Replying to OP would feel too direct / promotional

### skip
Best when:
- Thread is >7 days old (low visibility)
- Multiple competitors already recommended
- OP is venting, not seeking solutions
- Community has strict anti-promotion rules
- Thread is locked or archived
- Your product doesn't genuinely solve the stated problem

## Tone Matching by Platform

### Reddit
- Match the subreddit's formality level
- Use first person ("I built...", "I've been using...")
- Lead with genuine help, product mention comes naturally after
- Never use marketing language or superlatives

### HackerNews
- Technical depth expected — explain how, not just what
- Acknowledge limitations honestly
- Reference architecture or approach, not just features
- "Show HN" context vs. organic discussion require different tones

## Risk Factors to Flag

- Account age / karma too low for the subreddit
- Same product mentioned recently in the community
- Thread has negative sentiment toward promotional content
- Moderator recently removed a similar recommendation
- Thread topic is sensitive (health, finance, legal)
