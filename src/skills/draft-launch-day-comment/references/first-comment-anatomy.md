# First-comment anatomy

What the pinned maker comment needs to do on Product Hunt launch day:

1. Give voters a reason to care beyond the tagline.
2. Establish the founder as a specific person, not a logo.
3. Invite a thread of replies so the launch page has activity.
4. Leave room for the commenters to ask things the FAQ can't cover.

## Example 1 — origin_story hook

> Three months ago I opened a spreadsheet to plan my launch. By the time I
> was done I had 1200 rows of content ideas, three different tracker
> accounts, and the uneasy feeling that marketing was eating the time I
> had reserved for shipping.
>
> ShipFlare came out of that exact morning. It doesn't help you plan
> faster — it does the planning for you, in your voice, so you can go
> back to the code. You approve 20 minutes a week and marketing happens.
>
> What's the one marketing task you'd happily pay to stop doing?

Why it works: specific opening detail (spreadsheet, 1200 rows), expands on
the tagline without repeating it, ends on a question the commenters can
actually answer.

## Example 2 — contrarian_claim hook

> Most marketing autopilots push you to post more. ShipFlare pushes you
> to post less — fewer, more specific posts that match how you actually
> sound, scheduled around the threads you'd have found anyway.
>
> It took me 4 months to build because getting the voice right is harder
> than getting the copy right. The first 12 tweets it wrote for me were
> fine. The 13th made me do a double-take — I had to check the draft
> wasn't one of mine from last year.
>
> If you've ever deleted an AI-generated draft because it didn't sound
> like you, what was the tell?

Why it works: names a category expectation, inverts it, and the "13th
tweet" detail gives the commenter a concrete anchor.

## Patterns that kill

- "Super excited to launch..." — flattens the hook.
- "Check it out!" — tells voters nothing.
- Bullet lists of features — this is a page for the product, the comment
  is for the person.
- Three thank-yous in the first paragraph — save those for the comment
  thread; the first comment is for the wedge.
- Ending on a statement — readers won't reply unless asked.

## Length rules

- Floor: 80 chars. Anything shorter reads as a drive-by.
- Ceiling: 1200 chars. PH truncates long comments with "see more"; anything
  past 1200 doubles the tap to expand and many voters skip.
- Paragraph count: 2-4. One paragraph reads as sloppy; 5+ reads as a blog
  post.

## Voice-block precedence

When `voiceBlock` is present, defer to it for tone, contractions, signature
phrases. The opening-hook selector still applies — voice shapes HOW the
hook is voiced, not WHICH hook.
