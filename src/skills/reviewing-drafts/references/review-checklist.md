# Draft Review Checklist

## The Six Checks

### 1. Relevance
- Does the reply address what the OP actually asked?
- Is it answering the right question or pivoting to something adjacent?
- Would the OP think "this is helpful" or "this doesn't answer my question"?

### 2. Value-First
- Are there 2-3 sentences of real help BEFORE any product mention?
- If you removed the product mention, would the reply still be worth posting?
- Is the helpful content substantive or just a throwaway setup?

### 3. Tone Match
- Does it match the subreddit/platform culture?
- Is the formality level right?
- Does it read like someone who actually participates in this community?

### 4. Authenticity
- Does it read like a human or a marketing bot?
- Any telltale signs: superlatives, buzzwords, excessive enthusiasm?
- Right length? (Too short = low effort, too long = suspicious)

### 5. FTC Compliance
- Affiliation disclosure present?
- Disclosure at the END, not buried in the middle?
- Disclosure is honest and clear?

### 6. Risk Assessment
- Would a moderator remove this?
- Does it look like spam?
- Is the product mention proportionate to the help provided?

## Common Reviewer Traps

- "It's well-written" ≠ "it's effective marketing"
- Content agent's high confidence is self-assessment, not ground truth
- Having a disclosure doesn't fix a fundamentally spammy reply
- "Natural product mention" — read it as a skeptical community member
